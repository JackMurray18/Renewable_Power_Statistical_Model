Project Question

  • Examine how the wind speed throughout the year is related across 31 North Eastern locations
    • Determine if the power from some location can reliably supplement the power at other locations
    • Critical for the renewable energy to replace fossil fuels
  • Difficulty: Retrieving and preprocessing the data
    • Several data files
    • Set Daily Averages
  • Complicated to Fit models
    • Slow and difficult to make changes

Example of Trends

  • Wind speed at location 1020062 (Central Ohio)
    • Correlated with the other inland location
    • Less correlated with coastal locations
    • Coastal wind power maybe higher when inland power is low

Model

\[ A\times(\cos(\frac{\pi*days}{182.5})+ A\times(\cos(\frac{\pi*days}{91.25})+ A\times(\cos(\frac{\pi*days}{45.625})+ A\times(\sin(\frac{\pi*days}{182.5})+ A\times(\sin(\frac{\pi*days}{91.25})+ A\times(\cos(\frac{\pi*days}{45.625})+ C \]

  • This generic function was fitted with hundreds of combinations of parameters
  • The performance was evaluated by measuring the error between model predictions and observations
  • Selection Criteria
    • Min RMSE
  • Repeated for all 31 locations

Model Error

  • The error was calculated for each observation and predicted value
    • Critical to appropriately model the error for the simulation

Viewing the distribution of errors for location 1079715 (Lake Erie)

\[ \hat f(x)= f(x)+\epsilon \]

  • Need to model the error
    • Normal Distribution:
      • Scaling and Centering variables
      • Most models have a normal distribution of N~(1,0)
    • Gamma Distribution:
      • Results from infrequent high wind days
      • Caused Positive Skewing

Model Visual: Observered Wind Speed

  • Model captures the sinusoidal change in wind speed each year
    • Winters have higher wind speeds than the summer
  • Issues
    • Positive errors:
      • Model strudels to predict the few very high average wind speeds in winter
    • Location’s with low wind speed:
      • More error - high and low wind speed
      • Less normal error

Model Visual: Simualted Wind Speeds

  • The next 4000 days of normalized wind speeds are simulated and plotted
    • The fitted Error distribution supplies the randomness for each day
  • Random Error
    • Normal distribution for most errors
      • Still simulated lower than expected wind speed
    • Gamma distribution used some percentage of the time for large positive errors
      • Function underestimates peak wind days
